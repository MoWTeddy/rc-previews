Asked, answered

The honest answers.

What does it cost?

£1,000 a month, on a twelve-month term - typically treated as a business expense. No joining fee, no success fee, and no percentage of your sale.

Who is Rare Company for?

Established British owners and directors who are serious about building a more valuable company that needs them less - whether a sale is five years away, further, or not the plan at all. You don't need to be selling to join. You do need to be ambitious, open, and willing to contribute to the people around you.

Is my company large enough?

There's no single revenue threshold - value, complexity and potential vary hugely between businesses. What matters is that you're running a serious company, facing real decisions, and building towards an outcome where the right advice, relationships and preparation could be worth multi-millions.

I'm not planning to sell. Is this still for me?

Yes. Almost everything that makes a company sellable - strong profit, a team that runs without you, clean numbers, a clear story - is exactly what makes it better to own. Build those and you get your time back now and the choice later. If a sale never tempts you, you own a stronger company. If one does, you're years ahead.

I've already exited. Why would I join?

Because you hold the thing every other member needs, and the room holds what you need next: what to build, back or buy, and people who understand what the years after a sale are actually like.

How are members and peer groups matched?

By business scale, stage and intent. The aim is to place you with owners who understand the weight of your decisions, while keeping enough difference in experience that someone in the group has already faced what's coming next for you. We also weigh personalities, sectors and any potential conflicts before placing anyone together.

Will any of my competitors be members?

You'll never knowingly be placed in a private peer group with a direct competitor. Where there's any commercial sensitivity, we discuss it with you before making an introduction or inviting you into a particular setting.

Is it genuinely private?

What your company is worth - and whether you might one day sell it - is about the most commercially sensitive information there is, so confidentiality isn't a feature of Rare Company - it's the precondition. No notes, no quotes, no exceptions.

Do I have to share my financial information?

You decide what you disclose, and when - though the more honest the context, the more useful the advice. For guidance from Rare Advisors, detailed information helps us give you the best answer. Members are expected to speak openly in their private group, but nothing leaves it without their permission.

How much time does membership take?

You're running a serious company, so we don't expect you to set aside time for us. Advice, introductions and support are there when you need them; experiences are yours to choose by interest and availability. Rare Company should give you time back, not consume it - that's half the point.

Is this exit advisory?

No - it's the alternative to it, and a better one. Advisory sells you one firm's playbook, at the end, for a retainer and a percentage of everything you've built. Membership surrounds you with owners who've actually done it - many of them, years earlier, for a flat fee. When a transaction eventually needs specialists, you appoint them from the Rare Directory, on members' recommendations and your own terms.

What if I already have lawyers, accountants and advisers?

Most members do. Rare Company doesn't replace trusted advisers - it helps you test their advice, spot the gaps, and bring in extra expertise when a decision falls outside their experience or incentives. One adviser gives you one view; Rare Company gives you a far wider field of expertise.

Will anyone try to sell to me?

No. Rare Company is built for owners, not as a lead-generation platform for professional-services firms. Outside advisers don't attend member dinners or see the membership. When specialist help is needed, you decide whether to engage anyone introduced to you.

How is this different from a networking group or business club?

Networking groups are built around meeting more people - often packed with unqualified "owners". Rare Company is built around knowing the right people properly, increasing the value of your company, and making better decisions before, during and after an exit. No referral targets, no pitches, no name badges. The value is in depth, relevance and trust.

How is this different from Vistage, EO or a traditional peer group?

The focus is more commercially specific: your time, your company's value, and the decisions that change both. Peer support is one part of membership, surrounded by specialist advice, value tracking, private experiences and a vetted network of people worth hiring. The goal isn't just to become a better leader - it's to own a more valuable company that needs you less.

Will you help me find a buyer?

We're not a business broker, and we won't manufacture a sale before the company or owner is ready. But membership helps you understand buyer appetite, prepare the company properly, choose the right advisers and handle serious approaches - and where appropriate, trusted introductions can be made through the wider network. If an unexpected offer lands, you don't work it out alone: we can quickly connect you with owners who've faced the same, and bring in specialist advice where needed. The decision stays yours - made with far more context.
