Reading notes. The Table and the Door are the two with a story only Rare Company can tell; the asterisk family is the punchiest in small sizes and pairs naturally with the footnote idea of an off-record membership — set in serif it avoids reading as an AI starburst. The Monogram is the safe harbour. In the letter-marks, the asterisk sits in gold at footnote height, which keeps it a detail rather than a shout. Whichever direction wins, a brand designer should cut the final artwork, and the name-plus-mark should get a UK trademark search before it goes on anything permanent.
