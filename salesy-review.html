The pattern we're fixing

The modesty tax

Three habits show up on nearly every page. None are wrong, exactly — they're just leaving money in the room.

  • 01Headings label instead of sell. "What membership includes." "Who it's for." These describe a section; they don't give the reader a reason to want it.
  • 02We pre-apologise for our best numbers. "Even a modest result…", "quietly guaranteed", "a small number of…". We shrink the thing right as we show it.
  • 03We ask permission instead of inviting action. "If this sounds like your room, ask us for a seat." Confident brands assume the reader belongs.

The line we won't cross. "More salesy" does not mean guru-y. No "unlock", "transform", "10x", no fake scarcity, no exclamation marks, no hype. The move is simply: lead with the outcome, cut one hedge per sentence, and let the numbers stand up straight. Confident, not loud.

Quick wins

Words & phrases to cut

These softeners repeat across every page. Each one hedges a claim we should just make. One decision covers the lot.

Site-wide
  • "genuinely help you" (appears ~5×)→"help you." Dropping "genuinely" stops us sounding unsure we can.
  • "quietly guaranteed"→"Guaranteed." Our single best sales asset shouldn't whisper. (See bigger bet below.)
  • "a small number of exited founders"→"exited founders." Smallness is implied; saying it shrinks the value.
  • "we'd argue a better one"→"a better one." Don't hedge the claim — make it.
  • "even a modest result"→cut "even." Never pre-apologise for a number.
  • "if the room can help"→"when we can help." Assume competence.
Bigger bets

Three worth a proper conversation

These are structural, not one-line swaps — more upside, but more to build. Flag interest and we'll mock them up properly.

Home · new "proof strip" under the hero
New elementA single scannable line of facts we already own:
£12k a year, not £150k+  ·  every £1 of profit ≈ £5 at sale  ·  90 days, money back

Why: the strongest facts are currently spread across three pages. A three-stat strip high on the home page does the selling in two seconds, for skimmers who never scroll to Pricing. Factual, not hypey — it's your own numbers, just louder.

Home + Membership · plain-English subtitles on the six names
Now — the reader has to decodeYour Circle · The Panel · The Black Book · The Minutes · Rare Experiences · Your Rare Value
Suggested — name + instant benefitThe Panel — any big decision, answered within a day by owners who've made it.
The Black Book — the advisers worth hiring, rated by members who paid them.
Your Rare Value — your sale price, tracked and climbing.

Why: the names are memorable and worth keeping — but a first-time visitor has to work to see the value in "The Black Book". A one-line benefit under each name lets the value land instantly, then the name gives it character. Best of both.

Home + Membership + Pricing · the guarantee
NowQuietly guaranteed. Twice.
SuggestedGuaranteed. Twice. In writing.

Why: risk-reversal is the single biggest lever on a high-consideration purchase like this — and ours is genuinely strong (90 days money-back and an annual value check). Labelling it "quietly" is the purest example of the modesty tax. Keep both clauses exactly; just stop apologising for how good the offer is. This is the one place to raise your voice.
