My read: after-the-Y reads more naturally as a footnote mark (the asterisk points back at the name it annotates — “there’s more here than we print”), which is the whole idea. Before-the-R is more unusual and a touch more “brand mark” than footnote. For the favicon, both work at small size; *R has a slightly more balanced silhouette because the asterisk sits in the R’s open top-left rather than floating off the stem. If consistency between wordmark and favicon is the priority, both should match — so it’s really one decision, made twice.
