Pricing

What it costs, and what it's worth.

What membership costs, what the default path costs, and what preparation returns at a multiple — kept deliberately separate. No blending, no fine print.

The cost

The whole of it.

That is the whole of it. The dinners and breakfasts, your table, the Panel, the Black Book, the Minutes and the annual valuation review all sit inside the fee. There is no joining fee, no success fee, and no percentage of anything you sell. Many members treat the fee as a business expense; take your accountant's advice, not ours.

The return

What preparation pays.

£50,000profit added or defended × 5a five-times multiple = £250,000at completion

Businesses sell at a multiple of profit, so every improvement you make between now and completion lands multiplied — pricing, key hires, owner-dependence, the shape of your revenue.

One avoided mistake — a bad earn-out, the wrong adviser, going to market a year too early — is worth more than a decade of dues. Against £12,000 a year, the question isn't whether membership pays for itself. It's how many times.

And preparation decides the biggest outcome of all: whether you complete. Most businesses that go to market never do. The prepared ones are the exception.
