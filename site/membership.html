Membership

Explore membership.

This page is the whole picture: what membership includes, how it works, and how a seat is offered. What it can't show you is the conversation at the table - some things stay in the room.

Part one

What membership includes.

Six things, one fee, one outcome: the value of your business on the day you sell.

No. 1

The Rooms

Quarterly · Across the UK

Exited founders at every table. No badges, no speakers selling from a stage, no notes taken. The conversations that change valuations happen over dinner, not at conferences. Once a year, we go further: a single occasion worth clearing the diary for.

Each table is composed, not booked. We seat a small number of owners at similar stages alongside at least one founder who has signed and banked, and we let the evening find the agenda that matters: valuation, buyers, timing, what the deal actually felt like from the inside. You leave with the two or three conversations you would never have found at a conference - and usually a number to think hard about.

No. 2

Your Table

Monthly · Six to eight owners · Chaired

A standing group of owners at a similar stage and scale, meeting privately every month. The same faces each time: people with full context on your business, your numbers and your intentions, who tell you the truth because they have nothing to sell you.

Groups are matched on scale, stage and intent - near enough to understand each other's numbers, varied enough that someone at the table has already faced what's coming for you. A chair keeps the session honest: one owner's situation examined properly at a time, and returned to the following month. It is the discipline of a board, held among people with no position to protect.

No. 3

The Panel

On request · Within 24 hours · In confidence

Sell now or in two years? Take the earn-out? Hire a managing director first? Which adviser? Send the question in, and we convene two or three members who have faced exactly that decision. A private call, no minutes kept, no fee attached.

It works like this. You write to us on a Tuesday: a buyer has approached you, unsolicited, and the number is flattering. By Wednesday you are on a call with members who have taken that call before - one who sold, one who chose to wait. What you do next is entirely yours. What it would have cost to learn the hard way is the point.

No. 4

The Black Book

Always open · Vetted · Rated by members

Corporate finance, legal, tax, wealth, communications. Every name vetted, every one rated by members who have actually paid their invoices. Advisers appear in the Black Book. They do not appear at dinner.

Every name arrives by recommendation, is vetted by us, and carries the judgement of members who have been through a deal with them - what they charged, what they were like when the pressure came on, whether they'd be appointed again. Advisers never attend the rooms and never see the membership. When you need one, you choose from a shortlist that took someone else a decade to learn.

No. 5

The Minutes

Monthly · Members only · In writing

A brief drawn from every room: what buyers are actually paying, where lenders are tightening, which structures are getting done, what sellers regret. Not news - what owners like you are seeing, months before it is written anywhere.

Each month we set down what the rooms are hearing - the multiples being discussed, the sectors moving, the terms buyers are pushing and sellers are conceding, what a member wishes they had known before heads of terms. It exists because this information lives in private conversation years before it reaches print, and because you shouldn't have to attend every dinner to hold the whole picture.

No. 6

Where You Stand

At joining · Revisited every year

Membership begins with a private valuation and exit-readiness review: what your business is worth today, what is suppressing that number, and the priorities that move it. We revisit it every year, so you know precisely what membership has been worth.

The first review establishes your baseline: an honest view of what your business would fetch today, where the value is being held back, and the three or four priorities that move the number most. Each year, we take the measure again. The point is not the report. It is that you always know the distance between today's number and the one you intend to sign for.

Part two

How it works.

Membership belongs to you, not to your company - the seat, the conversations and the confidences are yours, and they stay yours through a sale and after it.

Your group is matched on stage, scale and intent, so the people around you understand your numbers without you having to translate. Tables meet across the UK, anchored in London, and everything runs under the same standing rule: what is said in the room stays in the room.

Monthly

Your table meets. The Minutes arrive. The picture sharpens.

Quarterly

A dinner or a breakfast, somewhere worth the journey, with people worth the seat.

Yearly

The occasion worth clearing the diary for - and your valuation, revisited.

Whenever needed

The Panel and the Black Book, on request, in confidence.

House rules

How the membership is run.

  1. i.In confidence

    What is said in the room stays in the room. No notes, no quotes, no exceptions.

  2. ii.Experience, not theory

    Every table is anchored by people who have done the thing, not studied it, advised on it or posted about it.

  3. iii.No pitching

    Nothing is sold in the room. Not services, not deals, not yourself. Nobody here wants your money.

  4. iv.By name

    There are no open tickets. Every seat is considered, and every member is there by name.

  5. v.Rare settings

    Private dining rooms, townhouses and tables you cannot book. The setting is part of the conversation.

Part three

Who it's for.

Owners and co-founders of established UK businesses - as a guide, £2 million or more in revenue - who intend to sell, in whole or in part, within the next five years. And a small number of exited founders, who sit on your side of the table for the company, not for a fee.

We keep the membership deliberately small, and we don't admit advisers, brokers, or anyone who would treat the room as a pipeline. Nothing is sold inside Rare Company, and nobody in the room wants your money.
